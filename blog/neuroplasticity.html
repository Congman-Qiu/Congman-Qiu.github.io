Research Growth Mindset

  • Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

    • Growth mindset is first studied by Carol Dweck and her teams.After studying the behavior of thousands of children, Dr. Dweck coined the terms fixed mindset and growth mindset to describe the underlying beliefs people have about learning and intelligence. When students believe they can get smarter, they understand that effort makes them stronger. Therefore they put in extra time and effort, and that leads to higher achievement.
    It is also related with the Neuroplasticity.These neuroscientific discoveries have shown us that we can increase our neural growth by the actions we take, such as using good strategies, asking questions, practicing, and following good nutrition and sleep habits. if you believe your brain can grow, you behave differently.This can affect your attitude towards learning and if you are willing to put more efforts on, making more practice, there’s more chance that you can have more achievements.

  • In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

    • I got inspired by The Power of … Yet after I watched the TED talk by Dweck where she describes “two ways to think about a problem that’s slightly too hard for you to solve.” People approach these problems with the two mindsets… “Are you not smart enough to solve it …. or have you just not solved it yet.In the past, sometimes I feel afraid to try some challenges especially for those things that totally new for me as I don’t like the feeling “I’m not smart for it”. While after knowing the growth mindset and the power of yet, I understand that it is not I’m not smart to solve it , it is I’m not reach that level yet but if I make efforts, do continuous practice, I will reach that stage in the near future.

  • How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

    • I will stop thinking like that “I’m not smart enough; It’s too hard for me.” I will change my thinking to something like “I understand that it may be a bit challenging for me but I will grow my ability to achieve.” For practice aspects, I will use break the tasks into small tasks which is slightly higher than my current level so I can learn it step by step. And I will code everyday as practice makes perfect.
